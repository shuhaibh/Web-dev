Albert Einstein

A RENOWNED PHYSICIST

Timeline of Einstein's Life:-

  • 1879: Albert Einstein is born on March 14 in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire.
  • 1896: Einstein graduates from high school in Aarau, Switzerland.
  • 1900: Einstein completes his studies at the Polytechnic in Zurich, Switzerland.
  • 1905: Einstein publishes four groundbreaking papers in physics, including the special theory of relativity, the photoelectric effect, and the relationship between mass and energy (E=mc²).
  • 1908: Einstein becomes a lecturer at the University of Bern in Switzerland.
  • 1915: Einstein presents the complete theory of general relativity, which revolutionizes our understanding of gravity.
  • 1919: Arthur Eddington's expedition confirms Einstein's prediction of light bending near massive objects, providing experimental evidence for general relativity.
  • 1921: Einstein is awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933: Due to the rise of Nazism in Germany, Einstein emigrates to the United States, where he takes a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
  • 1939: Einstein writes a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning about the potential of nuclear weapons and urging research into atomic energy for peaceful purposes.
  • 1940s-1950s: Einstein continues to work on unified field theories and quantum mechanics but does not achieve the breakthroughs he had hoped for.
  • 1955: Albert Einstein dies on April 18 in Princeton, New Jersey, at the age of 76.
